I am a fifth-year PhD candidate in the Department of Mathematics at Louisiana State University, advised by Zhiyu Wang. My research sits at the intersection of three areas: extremal and structural graph theory, applications of machine learning to combinatorial problems, and undergraduate mathematics education.

On the graph theory side I am interested in problems about how local structure forces global behavior — Ramsey-type questions, saturation problems, and the way random graph processes evolve. On the machine learning side I think about how learned models can be used as a tool for combinatorial discovery: generating conjectures, finding extremal examples, and helping us see structure we would otherwise miss.

On the education side I am interested in how students at the transition-to-proof stage develop a sense of what counts as a valid mathematical argument, and in how the way we teach calculus shapes what students believe mathematics is for.

Before LSU I studied mathematics in Nepal. Outside of math I read, run, and play chess (badly).
